We have a poultry problem.

99% of all poultry is factory farmed[1]

At the same time we are eating more chicken than ever before[2]

Chicken produced in the U.S. per year (In metric tons)

More than 8 billion chickens and turkeys are consumed in the U.S. every year[3]

Egg-laying hens have less space than a sheet of paper on which to spend their entire lives.[4]

Eight birds are crammed together in a cage the size of a file cabinet drawer.

Virtually all chickens and turkeys raised for meat have been bred to grow as large as possible in the shortest amount of time.[5]

As a result of modern breeding techniques,

Chickens and turkeys suffer unnecessay and painful problems with skeletal development[6], heart and lung function[7], obesity[8], and more.

What about the environment?

“Factory farms are among the biggest polluters on the planet.”

―Worldwatch institute, The Sierra Club, The Pew Charitable Trusts, Greenpeace

At least 15% of global greenhouse gas emissions come from animal agriculture.[13]

Mark Bittman has stated that if Americans reduced meat consumption by just 20% it would have the same impact as everyone in the U.S. switching from a sedan to a hybrid.[14]

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency found that chicken slaughterhouses can use more than 9 gallons of water to process each bird and that's not including the water used to grow crops to feed the birds, and water the animals drink.[15]
